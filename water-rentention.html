Water Retaining Structures Waterproofing

Waterproofing for water-retaining structures is essential to prevent water leakage, maintain structural integrity, and ensure safety by minimizing mold and health hazards. This process is critical for various structures, including reservoirs, tanks, swimming pools, basements, and septic tanks.

Importance

  • Prevent Water Leakage: Keeps stored water from escaping, reducing operational costs.
  • Protect Structural Integrity: Prevents damage from water infiltration, such as cracking and corrosion.
  • Health and Safety: Reduces risks of mold and damp-related health issues.

Common Structures

  • Reservoirs and Water Tanks: Store drinking, irrigation, or industrial water.
  • Swimming Pools: Maintain water levels without leaks.
  • Basements: Prevent groundwater infiltration in below-grade areas.
  • Septic Tanks: Hold and treat sewage waste, requiring effective waterproofing to prevent leaks that could contaminate surrounding soil and groundwater.

Waterproofing Techniques

  1. Membrane Systems: Flexible sheets (e.g., PVC, TPO, EPDM) provide a waterproof barrier, installed through adherence, mechanical fastening, or ballasting.
  2. Cementitious Coatings: Mixtures of cement and additives create a waterproof layer, suitable for concrete tanks and pools.
  3. Polyurethane/Polyurea Coatings: Seamless liquid-applied coatings ideal for complex surfaces.
  4. Bituminous Coatings: Durable waterproofing for below-grade applications, combining asphalt with additives.
  5. Injection Grouting: Fills cracks and voids with waterproof grout to seal potential leaks.

Surface Preparation

  • Cleaning: Remove debris and loose material for a clean surface.
  • Repairing Cracks: Fix visible cracks before applying waterproofing.
  • Drying: Ensure the substrate is dry to enhance adhesion.

Drainage Considerations

Integrate effective drainage systems (e.g., weep holes, drainage pipes) to manage hydrostatic pressure and prevent water accumulation against the structure.

Maintenance

Regular inspections and upkeep are crucial for long-term waterproofing effectiveness, including checking for damage or signs of water ingress.
