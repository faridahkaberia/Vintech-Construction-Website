Waterproofing Methods

Cementitious waterproofing involves the application of cement-based compounds to create a waterproof barrier. Used for water retaining structures like water tanks and basements.

Easy to apply, cost-effective, and provides a strong durable barrier.

A liquid-applied membrane made from materials like polyurethane or acrylic, forming a seamless, flexible coating. Often used on flat roofs and balconies.

Provides a seamless finish, adaptable to complex surfaces, and highly flexible.

Also known as asphalt coating, a black tar-like substance used to coat surfaces to create a protective barrier. It's mainly used on foundations and basement walls.

Excellent adhesion to various surfaces and good resistance to water.

A type of bitumen membrane reinforced with polymers, used for waterproofing flat and sloped roofs. It provides flexibility and durability under extreme weather conditions.

UV resistant, flexible, and durable.

Uses a fluid-like material injected into cracks or porous structures to fill gaps and stop water ingress. Used to repair leaks in concrete structures, tunnels, and underground basements.

Targets specific problematic areas, easy to apply in confined spaces.

Uses chemical treatments to penetrate concrete, react with water and calcium to form crystals that block pores and cracks. Used for basements, water tanks, and other underground structures.

Seals cracks over time and is highly effective in high-moisture areas.
